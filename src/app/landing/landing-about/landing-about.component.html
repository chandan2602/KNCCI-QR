About KNCCI

The Kenya National Chamber of Commerce and Industry is registered as a not-for-profit private company limited by guarantee under the Companies Act Chapter 486 of the Laws of Kenya. Before the promulgation of the Kenyan Constitution in August 2010, the KNCCI had its main operations at the head office in Nairobi with regional offices in all 47 counties in Kenya. However, after the promulgation of the constitution, the Chamber extended its large operations to establish a National Office with the devolved county chambers.

The Kenya National Chamber of Commerce and Industry (KNCCI) is a membership based trade support institution (TSI) working to protect commercial and industrial interests of Kenyan business community. KNCCI advocates for the creation of a favorable business, trade and investment environment that supports enterprise expansion. The membership of KNCCI constitutes small, micro enterprises (SMEs), medium and large enterprises.

Vision

A vibrant and prosperous business community

Mission

To initiate and promote a sustainable business environment for economic growth and prosperity

At the Kenya National Chamber of Commerce and Industry (KNCCI), integrity is a cornerstone of our mission to promote, protect, and develop the interests of the Kenyan business community. We are committed to upholding the highest ethical standards in all our operations, interactions, and decisions.

KNCCI provides strong protection against malpractices and ensures all reports are treated seriously and investigated independently. We also uphold strict safeguarding principles to protect vulnerable individuals across all our programs and partnerships.

To raise a concern anonymously or in confidence, contact us at web@kenyachamber.org or call us on "Private & Confidential – Whistleblower Concern".

KNCCI Functions

Promote, coordinate and protect commercial and industrial interest to its members and Kenya in general.

Collect and disseminate statistically sound information and other materials to its members

Promote trade within and outside Kenya

Arbitrate in the settlement of commercial and industrial disputes among its members

Establish and organize related trade and industries exhibitions and displays either on its own or in participation with other persons or organizations

Establish commercial exchanges, new rooms, libraries and other facilities that may be beneficial to its members

Foster social unity and promote the welfare of the commercial and industrial community

Provide facilities for the study, enquiry and research into commercial and industrial matters and to publish relevant and suitable writing others for the benefit of its members

Promote, support or oppose legislations and ineffective bureaucratic measures that may be put in place by the government for the interest of the members.
